# Source Coverage

This is the operator view for deciding what to improve next. It is not a promise that every public
DC data source belongs in the release.

Tiers:

- Tier 0: needed for a credible first civic-structure release.
- Tier 1: high-value next improvement.
- Tier 2: useful completeness expansion.
- Tier 3: future or out of scope for now.

Current stress-test baseline: a June 1, 2026 release-shaped workbench had all 13 configured sources
fetched, refreshed Council committee pages against the current parser, accepted 331 safe entity
candidates, accepted guarded Tier 0 relationship and legal-ref slices, and built a clean release.
The DCGIS `Other` branch bucket was skipped by safe relationship batching and explicitly deferred.
The compact release had 330 accepted entities, 228 accepted relationships, 30 datasets, 87 accepted
legal refs, and 18 pending legal refs; the remaining review queue was still large at 439 open items,
with 101 explicitly deferred review items and no source-status review items for successful captures.

## Tier 0 Gaps

1. Review or split the deferred DCGIS `Other` branch bucket without claiming relationship
   completeness.
2. Normalize or defer the remaining 50 open Council oversight endpoint rows without creating
   placeholders for funds, boards, programs, or unmatched non-agency concepts.
3. Keep Open DC legal-authority relationship placeholders deferred until legal refs have a real
   relationship/release path.
4. Normalize deferred ambiguous legal refs only where the source evidence supports it, especially
   DCGIS reorganization-plan, act-name, and law-number citations.
5. Decide whether Tier 0 needs a Board of Ethics / Open Government source lane before first usable
   release, or whether `dcgis.agencies`, Open DC, Council, and legal entrypoints are enough for the
   first release claim.

Next lane recommendation: decide whether the remaining open Council oversight endpoints should be
closed through a public-body endpoint normalization lane, a narrower synonym batch, or explicit
deferrals for funds/programs/regional bodies; then decide whether Council oversight or deferred
DCGIS residuals block a first useful release.
