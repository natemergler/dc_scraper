# Source Coverage

This is the operator view for deciding what to improve next. It is not a promise that every public
DC data source belongs in the release.

Tiers:

- Tier 0: needed for a credible first civic-structure release.
- Tier 1: high-value next improvement.
- Tier 2: useful completeness expansion.
- Tier 3: future or out of scope for now.

Current stress-test baseline: a June 1, 2026 release-shaped workbench had all 13 configured sources
fetched, refreshed Council committee pages against the current parser, accepted 334 safe entity
candidates, accepted guarded Tier 0 relationship and legal-ref slices, and built a clean release.
The DCGIS `Other` branch bucket was skipped by safe relationship batching and explicitly deferred.
The compact release had 333 accepted entities, 245 accepted relationships, 30 datasets, 89 accepted
legal refs, and 18 pending legal refs; the remaining review queue was still large at 390 open items,
with 136 explicitly deferred review items and no source-status review items for successful captures.

Fresh June 2, 2026 structure-lane smoke on a new temp workbench fetched `dcgis.agencies`,
`dcgis.boards_commissions_councils`, `open_dc.public_bodies`, `mota.quickbase`, `council.members`,
`council.committees`, `council.lims`, `oanc.anc_profiles`, `legal.entrypoints`, and
`admin.service_requests_311`; safe entity review accepted 333 items, follow-up safe legal and
relationship batches resolved the Councilmember/committee/ANC/public-body slice, and the release
built with 326 accepted entities, 171 accepted relationships, 78 accepted legal refs, and 209
`entity_legal_refs` rows. This was a structure-lane subset, not a full pass across every configured
source family.

## Tier 0 Gaps

1. Review or split the deferred DCGIS `Other` branch bucket without claiming relationship
   completeness.
2. Reconcile exact overlaps across DCGIS BCC, Open DC, Quickbase, Councilmembers, and ANC lanes so
   duplicate public bodies stay source-backed instead of being asserted silently.
3. Keep Open DC legal-authority relationship placeholders deferred until legal refs have a real
   relationship/release path.
4. Normalize deferred ambiguous legal refs only where the source evidence supports it, especially
   DCGIS reorganization-plan, act-name, and law-number citations.
5. Decide whether ANC stays Tier 1 or is promoted into Tier 0 after the overlap review, and whether
   any remaining Council role labels need a separate release policy before the first release claim.

Next lane recommendation: finish `#63` by settling the seat/authority review path and overlap policy
for Quickbase-backed public bodies, then build `#64` public appointee observations on top of that
seat model without introducing directory behavior.
